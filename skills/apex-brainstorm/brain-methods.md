# Brain Methods Reference

Technique library for apex-brainstorm. Load on-demand when presenting techniques to user.

## Collaborative (5 techniques)

1. **Yes And Building** - Build momentum through positive additions. Each idea becomes a launching pad. Use "Yes, and we could also..." to create energetic collaborative flow.
2. **Brain Writing Round Robin** - Silent idea generation followed by building on others' written concepts. Gives quieter voices equal contribution through write-pass-build cycles.
3. **Random Stimulation** - Use random words/images as creative catalysts to force unexpected connections. Breaks through mental blocks with serendipitous inspiration.
4. **Role Playing** - Generate solutions from multiple stakeholder perspectives. Embody different roles: what do they want, how would they approach it, what matters most to them.
5. **Ideation Relay Race** - Rapid-fire idea building under time pressure. 30-second additions, quick building, fast passing. Creates urgency and breakthroughs by preventing overthinking.

## Creative (11 techniques)

1. **What If Scenarios** - Explore radical possibilities by questioning all constraints. "What if we had unlimited resources?" "What if the opposite were true?" "What if this problem didn't exist?"
2. **Analogical Thinking** - Find solutions by drawing parallels to other domains. "This is like what?" "How is this similar to..." Transfer successful patterns from elsewhere.
3. **Reversal Inversion** - Flip problems upside down to reveal hidden assumptions. "What if we did the opposite?" "How could we make this worse?" Great when conventional approaches fail.
4. **First Principles Thinking** - Strip away assumptions to rebuild from fundamental truths. "What do we know for certain?" "If we started from scratch?" Essential for breakthrough innovation.
5. **Forced Relationships** - Connect unrelated concepts to spark innovative bridges. Take two unrelated things, find connections, identify bridges, explore how they work together.
6. **Time Shifting** - Explore solutions across different time periods. "How would this work in the past?" "100 years from now?" Reveals constraints and opportunities.
7. **Metaphor Mapping** - Use extended metaphors as thinking tools. Transform abstract challenges into tangible narratives. Map elements to discover insights.
8. **Cross-Pollination** - Transfer solutions from completely different industries or domains. How would industry X solve this? What patterns work in field Y?
9. **Concept Blending** - Merge two or more existing concepts to create entirely new categories. Goes beyond combination to genuine innovation. What new category is created?
10. **Reverse Brainstorming** - Generate problems instead of solutions. "What could go wrong?" "How could we make this fail?" Reveals solution insights through inversion.
11. **Sensory Exploration** - Engage all five senses to discover multi-dimensional solution spaces. What does this idea feel, smell, taste, sound like? Beyond purely analytical thinking.

## Deep Analysis (8 techniques)

1. **Five Whys** - Drill through layers of causation to uncover root causes. Ask "Why did this happen?" repeatedly until reaching fundamental drivers.
2. **Morphological Analysis** - Systematically explore all parameter combinations. Identify key parameters, list options for each, try combinations, find patterns.
3. **Provocation Technique** - Use deliberately provocative statements to extract useful ideas from absurd starting points. "What if [provocative statement]?" Extract the principle.
4. **Assumption Reversal** - Challenge and flip core assumptions. "What assumptions are we making?" "What if the opposite were true?" Essential for paradigm shifts.
5. **Question Storming** - Generate questions before seeking answers. Only questions, no answers yet. Focus on what we don't know. Ensures solving the right problem.
6. **Constraint Mapping** - Identify and visualize all constraints. Which are real vs imagined? How to work around or eliminate barriers?
7. **Failure Analysis** - Study successful failures. What went wrong, why it failed, what lessons emerged, how to apply failure wisdom to current challenges.
8. **Emergent Thinking** - Allow solutions to emerge organically. What patterns emerge? What wants to happen naturally? Embrace complexity and natural development.

## Introspective (6 techniques)

1. **Inner Child Conference** - Channel childhood curiosity and wonder. What would 7-year-old you ask? Use "why why why" questioning. Make it fun. Forbid boring thinking.
2. **Shadow Work Mining** - Explore what you're actively avoiding. Where's resistance? What scares you? Mine the shadows for buried wisdom.
3. **Values Archaeology** - Excavate deep personal values. What really matters? Why do you care? What's non-negotiable? Dig to bedrock motivations.
4. **Future Self Interview** - Seek wisdom from your future self. What would 80-year-old you say? What does long-term perspective reveal?
5. **Body Wisdom Dialogue** - Let physical sensations guide ideation. What does your body say? Where do you feel it? Trust tension. Follow physical cues.
6. **Permission Giving** - Grant explicit permission to think impossible thoughts. Break self-imposed creative barriers. Explore, try, experiment freely.

## Structured (7 techniques)

1. **SCAMPER Method** - Seven lenses: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse. Systematic product improvement and innovation.
2. **Six Thinking Hats** - Six perspectives: White (facts), Red (emotions), Yellow (benefits), Black (risks), Green (creativity), Blue (process). Comprehensive analysis without conflict.
3. **Mind Mapping** - Branch ideas from central concept. Put main idea in center, branch concepts, identify sub-branches. Visual connections and big picture.
4. **Resource Constraints** - Impose extreme limitations. What if only $1? No technology? One hour? Forces essential priorities and creative efficiency.
5. **Decision Tree Mapping** - Map all possible decision paths and outcomes. Identify decision points and where different choices lead.
6. **Solution Matrix** - Create grid of problem variables and solution approaches. Test combinations, find optimal pairings, discover gaps.
7. **Trait Transfer** - Borrow attributes from successful solutions in unrelated domains. What traits make success X work? How to transfer them here?

## Theatrical (6 techniques)

1. **Time Travel Talk Show** - Interview past/present/future selves. Playful temporal wisdom gathering across different life stages.
2. **Alien Anthropologist** - Examine problems through completely foreign eyes. Become alien observer. What seems strange? Reveals hidden assumptions.
3. **Dream Fusion Laboratory** - Start with impossible fantasy solutions, then reverse-engineer practical steps. Dream impossible, work backwards to reality.
4. **Emotion Orchestra** - Let different emotions lead separate sessions then harmonize. Angry perspective, joyful approach, fearful considerations, hopeful solutions.
5. **Parallel Universe Cafe** - Explore solutions under alternative reality rules. Different physics, alternative social norms, changed historical events.
6. **Persona Journey** - Embody different archetypes. Become the archetype. How would this persona solve it? What does the character see that normal thinking misses?

## Wild (8 techniques)

1. **Chaos Engineering** - Deliberately break things. What if everything went wrong? Build anti-fragility by stress-testing against worst-case scenarios.
2. **Guerrilla Gardening Ideas** - Plant unexpected solutions in unlikely places. Where's the least expected place? Stealth innovation through surprise.
3. **Pirate Code Brainstorm** - Take what works from anywhere and remix without permission. Maverick thinking. Need no permission.
4. **Zombie Apocalypse Planning** - Design for extreme survival. What basics work when society collapses? Strip to essential functions and core value.
5. **Drunk History Retelling** - Explain complex ideas with uninhibited simplicity. No filter. Raw thoughts. Simplify to absurdity to find raw truth.
6. **Anti-Solution** - Generate ways to make the problem worse. Sabotage it. Make it fail spectacularly. Destructive creativity reveals hidden assumptions.
7. **Quantum Superposition** - Hold multiple contradictory solutions simultaneously. How can all solutions be true at once? What happens when you observe?
8. **Elemental Forces** - Solutions sculpted by natural elements. How would earth sculpt this? What would fire forge? How does water flow through? What does air reveal?

## Biomimetic (3 techniques)

1. **Nature's Solutions** - How nature solves similar problems. 3.8 billion years of evolutionary wisdom. What ecosystems provide parallels?
2. **Ecosystem Thinking** - Analyze problem as ecosystem. Symbiotic relationships, natural succession, ecological principles for systems thinking.
3. **Evolutionary Pressure** - Apply evolutionary principles. What selective pressures apply? How does this adapt over time?

## Quantum (3 techniques)

1. **Observer Effect** - How observing and measuring solutions changes their behavior. How to use observer effect advantageously?
2. **Entanglement Thinking** - How different elements might be connected regardless of distance. What hidden connections exist?
3. **Superposition Collapse** - Hold multiple potential solutions until constraints force single optimal outcome. What constraints force collapse?

## Cultural (4 techniques)

1. **Indigenous Wisdom** - Draw upon traditional knowledge systems. How would specific cultures approach this? What ancestral wisdom applies?
2. **Fusion Cuisine** - Mix cultural approaches like fusion cuisine. What cultural hybrids emerge? Innovation through cross-pollination.
3. **Ritual Innovation** - Apply ritual design principles. What ritual would transform this? How to make it ceremonial?
4. **Mythic Frameworks** - Use myths and archetypal stories as frameworks. What myth parallels this? What archetypes are involved?
